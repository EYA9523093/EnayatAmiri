About

I was born in 1998 in Chaharmahal and Bakhtiari, Iran. I received my B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering, specializing in Communication Engineering, from Isfahan University of Technology in 2021. During this time, I broadened my knowledge of Medical Engineering and communication systems. Building on this foundation, I pursued my M.Sc. in Electrical Engineering with a focus on Control Engineering at the Sharif University of Technology. Under the supervision of Prof. Mohammad Haeri, my research centers on scheduling traffic lights using secure computation. I successfully completed my degree in 2024. Throughout my master’s studies, I enhanced both my research background and soft skills, working on traffic systems and employing cryptographic techniques to secure traffic signals.
Over the course of my academic journey, I authored two research papers: one presented at the International Convergence of Electrical Engineering (ICEE) conference and another published in the Journal of IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems, reflecting my growing expertise and contributions in the field. I currently serve as a researcher in the Department of Electrical Engineering at the Sharif University of Technology. My primary research interests include transportation systems, cyber-physical systems, model predictive control, and multi-agent systems. Through my work, I aim to advance innovative solutions that bridge the gap between theoretical control concepts and their real-world applications in intelligent transportation and beyond.
